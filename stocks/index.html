Editorial — the markets page

Treat the scan like the back page of the FT. Big serif masthead, three columns of tabular picks, a lede with the day's headline number. Calm, considered, no chrome. The opposite of "dashboard energy."

Why this works

  • Forces a real information hierarchy — the lede tells you what matters
  • Three columns = picks / movers / watchlist, no scrolling between
  • Charts shrink to rule-thin sparklines that don't fight the type
  • Iconography becomes typographic glyphs (†, ‡, §)

Heat Grid — the whole tape at a glance

Treemap-style. Cell size = conviction or volume; cell color = direction × magnitude. The top 3 picks get 2×2 cells. You see all 30+ items in one pane and tap to drill in. Most pre-attentive of the four.

vibe · trading-floor  ·  density · 8/10  ·  mode · dark

Why this works

  • Color does 80% of the cognitive work
  • Ranking is spatial, not numeric — biggest cell = matters most
  • Sections stack: Picks · Movers · Watchlist as separate grids
  • Iconography fits in 12px corner — never crowds
